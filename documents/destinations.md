# Destination Guides

## Kyoto, Japan
Kyoto is Japan's former imperial capital, known for classical Buddhist temples,
gardens, imperial palaces, Shinto shrines, and traditional wooden houses. It is
ideal for travelers interested in culture, history, and relaxed walking tours.
Best visited in spring (cherry blossoms, late March-April) or autumn (foliage,
November). Public transit (bus and train) is affordable and extensive; many
temple districts like Higashiyama are best explored on foot or by bicycle.

## Reykjavik, Iceland
Reykjavik is a compact, walkable capital and the gateway to Iceland's natural
wonders (Golden Circle, Blue Lagoon, glaciers, waterfalls). Self-drive rental
cars are the most popular way to explore beyond the city due to limited public
transit outside the capital. Best visited June-August for long daylight, or
September-March for the northern lights.

## Lisbon, Portugal
Lisbon offers historic neighborhoods, coastal day trips (Sintra, Cascais), and
a mild climate year-round. The city's trams, metro, and funiculars make it
easy to get around without a car; walking is pleasant in the hilly old town.
Good for food lovers, budget travelers, and couples.

## Queenstown, New Zealand
Queenstown is an adventure-travel hub on Lake Wakatipu, popular for hiking,
bungee jumping, skiing, and scenic road trips. A rental car or organized tours
are recommended since many attractions are outside town; the town center
itself is very walkable.
