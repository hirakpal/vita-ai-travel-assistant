# Transportation Guides

## General Principles
- Walking is preferred for local distances under ~5 km when paths are safe and
  weather permits.
- Bicycles suit flat, mid-distance urban trips (5-15 km) in bike-friendly
  cities (Amsterdam, Copenhagen, Kyoto).
- Buses and metro are cost-effective for city and short regional trips.
- Trains suit medium-to-long domestic distances (100-800 km) with good rail
  infrastructure (Japan, Europe).
- Self-drive rental cars suit regions with limited public transit or
  multi-stop road trips (Iceland, New Zealand, US national parks).
- Taxis and ride-hailing suit late-night travel, heavy luggage, or
  accessibility needs.
- Flights suit international or long-haul (800+ km) travel.
- Multi-modal itineraries (e.g., flight + train + walking) often provide the
  best balance of cost, comfort, and experience.

## Regional Notes
- Japan: extensive, punctual train network (JR Pass for tourists); walking and
  bicycles work well within cities.
- Iceland: very limited public transit outside Reykjavik; self-drive is
  standard for exploring the Ring Road.
- Western Europe: high-speed rail connects most major cities efficiently;
  budget airlines are common for longer hops.
- Southeast Asia: mix of budget flights, trains, and ride-hailing apps (Grab)
  for local trips.
