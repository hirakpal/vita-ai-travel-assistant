# Travel Tips, Packing, and Etiquette

## Packing Suggestions
- Weather-appropriate layered clothing based on destination and season.
- Universal power adapter and a portable charger.
- Comfortable walking shoes for cities with heavy walking itineraries.
- Copies (digital and physical) of passport, visas, and travel insurance.

## Cultural Etiquette
- Japan: remove shoes indoors, avoid tipping, be quiet on public transit.
- Iceland: tipping is not expected; respect posted trail boundaries near
  geothermal areas.
- Portugal: greet with a friendly "bom dia"; meals are often later in the
  evening than in other parts of Europe.

## Currency & Visa Notes
- Always check current visa requirements for the traveler's passport and
  destination; requirements change over time and should not be assumed.
- Carry a small amount of local currency for cash-only vendors even in
  countries that are largely cashless.

## Weather Guidance
- Shoulder seasons (spring/autumn) often offer good weather with fewer crowds
  than peak summer or holiday periods.
- Always check destination-specific seasonal patterns (monsoon, hurricane
  season, extreme heat/cold) before finalizing dates.
